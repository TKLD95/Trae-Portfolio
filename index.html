Year 3

Immersive Dual-Rig Entertainment System

The Context: Commercial 360-camera booths at live events often suffer from high software subscription costs, long post-production wait times, and a rapid loss of audience interest. The objective was to innovate live event entertainment by creating a cost-effective, multipurpose interactive rig.

My Role: Lead Interactive Designer. I engineered a dual-function entertainment system that keeps users engaged by eliminating wait times and offering real-time, customizable visual content. I successfully integrated a live feed from a 360-camera booth with an interactive Kinect rig.

The Process:

  • Developed real-time visual content for both a 360-camera rig and a Kinect interactive rig using TouchDesigner.
  • Built complex audio-reactive environments ("Outrun", "Moving Space", "How Deep Is Your Love") utilizing nodes like Phong, Feedback Edge, and Noise.
  • Leveraged Xbox Kinect v2 point cloud, depth, and color indices to allow users to physically manipulate digital projections (e.g., glitch effects, particle systems).
  • Synchronized a virtual camera node to match a physical 360-booth arm's speed, placing users inside seamless, interchangeable UV-mapped worlds.

Screens Project - Skimmr

The Context: The Skimmr project involved the production of a title sequence for a film set in a passive-aggressive corporate office environment. The shoot required executing a precise shot list across multiple locations, including exterior entrances, security gates, and a studio meeting room, to establish the film's narrative tone and reveal the company branding.

My Role: Digital Imaging Technician (DIT). I served as the essential bridge between the artistic vision of the filmmakers and the technical demands of digital cinematography, safeguarding footage and ensuring a seamless transition from shooting to post-production.

The Process:

  • Organized, backed up, and verified all digital footage to ensure no data was lost and files were perfectly prepped for post-production.
  • Applied temporary color grades and on-set adjustments to match the cinematographer’s vision, ensuring visual consistency across all scenes.
  • Provided strict technical oversight by monitoring camera settings, resolution, aspect ratio, codecs, LUTs, and frame rates to preserve the creative integrity of the digital footage.
  • Acted as the primary workflow coordinator and liaison between the production and post-production teams to facilitate a smooth handoff of footage and metadata.

Year 2

Sights, Sounds, and Serotonin

The Context: A fun, interactive exploration into the relationship between seeing, hearing, and feeling, and how basic movements can produce serotonin. The goal was to create panels that combine physical audience interaction with visually stimulating images and music to promote mental well-being.

My Role: Lead Interactive Designer. I developed three distinct interactive environments that tracked audience movement and reacted to audio inputs to evoke specific emotional responses.

The Process:

  • Designed three unique experiential panels utilizing TouchDesigner and an Xbox Kinect sensor.
  • Mapped low, mid, and high audio harmonics to visuals so the environments would dynamically react to specific songs.
  • Converted audience body movements into data (like optic flow) to allow physical manipulation of the digital environments.

Screen Production 1: The Graduate & The Card

The Context: The project involved the back-to-back filming of two short films, The Graduate and The Card. The production required complex multi-location shoots, including a TV studio, a basement dance floor, and office spaces, necessitating a highly adaptable and reliable live video monitoring setup.

My Role: Monitor and Playback Engineer. I was responsible for setting up and managing the on-set monitoring and playback systems. I ensured the director, Director of Photography (DOP), and crew could view live feeds and recorded takes in real-time for immediate feedback, while troubleshooting any technical video feed issues.

The Process:

  • Designed and built a compact, mobile "video village" on a two-tier trolley to accommodate rapid location changes and small set spaces.
  • Configured and managed wireless encoders, receivers, SDI distribution, and ATEM switchers to route high-definition video to multiple department monitors.
  • Overcame distance and signal blocking challenges by utilizing an NDI (Network Device Interface) encoder to patch camera feeds through the building's internal network to an NDI receiver.
  • Monitored timecode synchronization across devices and maintained detailed manual shot logs to assist the Digital Imaging Technician (DIT) with syncing audio and video for dailies.

Academic Background: Digital Design & Production

The Digital Design and Production course is a comprehensive program that blends theoretical performance analysis with practical technical training. Students evaluate various contemporary live performances and exhibitions to study elements like set design, lighting, costumes, and audience reception. The practical curriculum trains students in stage mechanics, overhead rigging, equipment maintenance, and associated studies in electrics and sound for theatre. A major focus is placed on digital content creation, where students learn to use industry-standard software such as Adobe After Effects, Premiere Pro, Illustrator, Houdini 3D, TouchDesigner, Resolume, DaVinci Resolve, and D3 (disguise). Through hands-on projects and technical workshops, students develop skills in 2D and 3D animation, motion capture, projection mapping, cinematography, and camera fundamentals. Furthermore, the course prepares students for the professional industry by teaching essential freelance business skills, including managing contracts, quoting, invoicing, and understanding taxes.
